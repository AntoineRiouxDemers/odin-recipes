Pizza

Description

Pizza is a popular and versatile dish that originated in Italy and has become a beloved food worldwide.
It typically consists of a flat, round dough base topped with a variety of ingredients and baked to perfection. Here's a general description of a classic pizza.

Ingredients

  • Pizza dough (homemade or store-bought)
  • 1 cup tomato sauce
  • 2 cups fresh mozzarella cheese, sliced or shredded
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cornmeal or flour for dusting

Steps

  1. Preheat the Oven:

    Preheat your oven to the highest temperature it can go, typically around 475-500°F (245-260°C).
    If you have a pizza stone, place it in the oven to heat up.

  2. Prepare the Dough:

    Roll out the pizza dough on a floured surface to your desired thickness.
    If you have a pizza peel, you can transfer the dough onto it. Otherwise, use a piece of parchment paper.

  3. Assemble the Pizza:

    Spread a thin layer of tomato sauce over the rolled-out dough, leaving a small border around the edges.
    Place the mozzarella slices or shredded mozzarella evenly over the sauce. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  4. Bake the Pizza:

    If using a pizza stone, carefully transfer the pizza onto the hot stone in the oven.
    If using parchment paper, slide the pizza and parchment paper onto a baking sheet or pizza pan.
    Bake in the preheated oven for about 10-12 minutes or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly and slightly browned.

  5. Finish and Garnish:

    Once out of the oven, drizzle a bit of olive oil over the pizza. Tear fresh basil leaves and scatter them on top.
    Allow the pizza to cool for a few minutes before slicing.

  6. Serve:

    Slice your Margherita pizza into wedges and serve hot. Enjoy the classic simplicity of this delicious pizza!
